# Financial Ratios – Clean Version

## ⚠️ Important Rule
When mixing:
- Balance Sheet (point in time)
- Income Statement (period)

👉 Use AVERAGE values for balance sheet items.

---

# 📊 Profitability Ratios

## ROE (Return on Equity)
Formula:
ROE = Net Income / Average Equity

Meaning:
Measures how much profit is generated from shareholders' money  
👉 Higher = better

---

## ROA (Return on Assets)
Formula:
ROA = Net Income / Average Total Assets

Meaning:
Measures how efficiently assets generate profit  
👉 Higher = better

---

## Net Profit Margin
Formula:
Net Profit Margin = Net Income / Sales

Meaning:
Profit generated per dollar of revenue  
👉 Higher = better

---

# 💧 Liquidity Ratios

## Current Ratio
Formula:
Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Meaning:
Ability to pay short-term obligations  
👉 Higher = safer (but too high = inefficient)

---

## Quick Ratio
Formula:
Quick Ratio = (Cash + Accounts Receivable) / Current Liabilities

Meaning:
More conservative liquidity measure  
👉 Higher = better

---

## Interest Coverage
Formula:
Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense

Meaning:
Ability to pay interest on debt  
👉 Higher = safer

---

# 🏦 Leverage Ratios

## Total Debt / Equity
Formula:
Debt to Equity = Total Liabilities / Total Equity

Meaning:
Measures financial leverage  
👉 Lower = less risky

---

## Long-term Debt / Equity
Formula:
Long-term Debt to Equity = Long-term Debt / Total Equity

Meaning:
Focuses only on long-term debt  
👉 Lower = less risky

---

# ⚙️ Efficiency Ratios

## Asset Turnover
Formula:
Asset Turnover = Sales / Average Total Assets

Meaning:
Efficiency in using assets to generate revenue  
👉 Higher = better

---

## Receivables Turnover
Formula:
Receivables Turnover = Sales / Average Accounts Receivable

Meaning:
How fast customers pay  
👉 Higher = better

---

## Inventory Turnover
Formula:
Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory

Meaning:
How quickly inventory is sold  
👉 Higher = better

---

## Fixed Assets Turnover
Formula:
Fixed Assets Turnover = Sales / Average PPE

Meaning:
Efficiency of fixed assets  
👉 Higher = better

---

# 📈 Market Ratios

## P/E Ratio
Formula:
P/E = Share Price / Earnings per Share

Meaning:
Market expectations about growth  
👉 High = growth or overvalued

---

## Price to Book
Formula:
Price to Book = Share Price / Book Value per Share

Meaning:
Comparison of market value vs accounting value  
👉 Lower often = undervalued

---

## Dividend Payout
Formula:
Dividend Payout = Dividends / Net Income

Meaning:
% of profit distributed to shareholders  
👉 Depends on strategy (not good/bad)
